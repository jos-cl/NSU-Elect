The Northern Technical College Students' Union Constitution

This page publishes the constitutional text supplied for the Northern Technical College Students' Union, including the preamble, governance structure, election rules, finances, and adoption record.

Union: Northern Technical College Student Union (NSU) Slogan: VIVA MWANA NSU Reviewed: 05/02/2015 to 05/03/2015

Preamble

We, the students of the Northern Technical College, aware and conscious of the great responsibility bestowed on us as a dynamic and energetic force on whom depends the future destiny of our nation(s) and on whom are invested the hopes of our fathers and mothers, and realising the vital need that students of the Northern Technical College be properly guided and groomed to take their right.

And convinced too, that it is only by serving effectively to have a full say in the administration of this College that students interests can be promoted and safeguarded.

Recognising the need therefore to create a new and dynamic student Government capable of carrying out a programme of action to reflect the desires and aspirations, do hereby resolve to give ourselves this constitution for the Northern Technical College Union regardless of gender, age, discipline or level of study, colour, race, creed or political affiliation.

Article 1: Name of the Union

The name of the union shall be Northern Technical College Student Union (NSU). The slogan shall be 'VIVA MWANA NSU'.

Article 2: Location

The Headquarters of NSU shall be at the Northern Technical College, Chela road, P O Box 250093, Ndola, Zambia.

Article 3: Aims and Objectives

  1. 3.1 To promote, protect and defend students' interests and rights within and outside the college.
  2. 3.2 To strive for better standards of education and academic freedom.
  3. 3.3 To provide facilities for recreation or other leisure occupations being such facilities as will enhance and promote social, economic, spiritual, cultural and intellectual development of students.
  4. 3.4 To work for the co-operation of students with the community at large and to foster development within students and responsibility towards society.
  5. 3.5 To promote and protect the general welfare of members.
  6. 3.6 To act as a channel of communication between its members and Northern Technical College Management, Government and other Authorities within and outside Zambia.
  7. 3.7 To promote, coordinate and facilitate the activities of lawful student interest groups.
  8. 3.8 To promote the principles and ideas of good governance in the Union, College and the Nation.
  9. 3.9 To work and participate in the development of a strong student movement within and outside the Union, College and the Nation at large.
  10. 3.10 To promote and participate in any activity incidental to all or any of the above.

Article 4: Membership

  1. 4.1 Membership of the Northern Technical College Students' Union shall be as follows:
    4.1.1 Full membership
    4.1.2 Associate membership
  2. 4.2 Full Membership
  3. 4.2.1 Membership to the Union shall be compulsory to all full-time, part-time and ODL students registered by the Northern Technical College.
  4. 4.2.2 All full members of the Union shall be eligible for membership of all clubs and societies registered with the Students Affairs Officer and the Union and shall enjoy the use of the buildings entrusted to Union by Nortec Management and facilities therein.
  5. 4.2.3 Full members shall have the right to vote in the Union elections, stand for elections to Union office subject to qualifications to contest for a particular portfolio and otherwise take part in the governance of the Union, its clubs and societies.
  6. 4.2.4 The Student Union shall levy membership fee against all full members of the Union each academic term as reviewed.
  7. 4.3 Associate Membership
  8. 4.3.1 Associate membership shall be open to all students of the College who are admitted to a course of study, the duration of which shall be one month but less than one year or twelve months.

Article 5: Bill of Student Rights

  1. 5.1 All students are considered equal and no student shall on the basis of race, language, gender, creed, ethnic descent, social status, disability, year, course, group or other natural characteristics, receive unfair privileges or be subjected to unfair discriminations.
  2. 5.2 Every student is entitled to the right of recognition and protection of his or her human dignity.
  3. 5.3 Each student has the right to privacy of his/her personal property.
  4. 5.4 Every student has the right to freedom of thought and religion without infringing on the right to freedom of religion, the practising of religion in the student structures may take place according to the rules and regulations of that organisation.
  5. 5.5 Every student has the right to academic freedom and training without interruption.
  6. 5.6 Every student has the right to freedom of movement as well as freedom of association.
  7. 5.7 Every student has the right of assembly, right to petition NORTEC management, and right to hold peaceful and unarmed demonstration after obtaining a police permit.
  8. 5.8 Every student has the right to information regarding decisions taken by student organisational structures.
  9. 5.9 Every student has the right to vote for students' structures, to do so in secret and make him or herself available for election to these structures subject to the provisions of this constitution.
  10. 5.10 Every student has the right to freedom of speech and to express an opinion as well as the right to listen to the opinion of others.
  11. 5.11 Every student has the right to safety.
  12. 5.12 Every student has a right to a clean environment, room, toilets etc.
  13. 5.13 Every student has an obligation to keep their environment and rooms clean.
  14. 5.14 Every student has a right to administrative fairness, which includes a fair trial.
  15. 5.15 All the above rights are limited by:
    5.15.1 The rights of other students;
    5.15.2 Reasonableness and fairness necessary in a democratic and academically oriented student community;
    5.15.3 The practical feasibility of these rights;
    5.15.4 The limitations on the jurisdiction of the Laws of the Republic of Zambia.
    5.15.5 The rules and regulations of the college.

Article 6: Organs of Governance in the Union

  1. 6.1 The organs of Governance in the Union shall be:
  2. 6.2.1 The General Meeting
  3. 6.2.2 The Union Executive
  4. 6.2.3 The Interim Executive
  5. 6.2.4 Disciplinary Committee

Article 7: General Meeting

  1. 7.1 There shall be a General meeting called by the NSU executive in consultation with the School Calendar during the fourth week of every term at which members shall be entitled to attend, speak and vote on motions tabled.
  2. 7.2 There shall be an Annual General Meeting (AGM) held in the fourth week of the 2nd Term at which:
  3. 7.2.1 The Treasurer General of NSU shall present an annual financial report provided that the internal auditor shall audit the accounts, such a report should be made public 7 days before the AGM.
  4. 7.2.2 The Secretary General shall present the asset report (i.e. asset register audit report).
  5. 7.2.3 Any other matter shall be considered for discussion.
  6. 7.3 Notice and agenda of every general and annual general meeting shall be communicated to the students four (4) working days before the meeting.
  7. 7.4 Extraordinary general meeting shall be called at anytime by the Union President or by petition by 50 members.
  8. 7.5 Public notice for an extra-ordinary meeting shall not be less than 8 hours and the agenda for such a meeting shall also be given.
  9. 7.6 The quorum for General and Annual General Meetings shall require:
  10. 7.6.1 1/2 members of the student body to be in attendance.
  11. 7.6.2 Extra-ordinary general meeting will require 1/2 members to be in attendance.
  12. 7.6.3 The General Meeting shall be the supreme policy making body of the Union.

Article 8: The Disciplinary Committee

  1. 8.1 The Disciplinary Committee shall be composed of the following:
  2. 8.1.1 One (1) student appointed by the Students Affairs Officer.
  3. 8.1.2 One student appointed by the NSU executive.
  4. 8.1.3 Five (5) representatives of the religious community: ZAFES, UCZ, CASC, SDA, JEHOVA.
  5. 8.2 The Disciplinary Committee shall operate independently but submit its findings to the NSU executive.
  6. 8.3 Any member of the Disciplinary may be removed by two-third (2/3) majority of NSU executive present at a general meeting called for the purpose.
  7. 8.4 The Disciplinary shall sit whenever need arises provided they meet at least once a month.
  8. 8.5 Membership of the Disciplinary shall be by way of appointment. Subject to this provision, a person appointed for this purpose may either accept or reject the offer within seven (7) days of receiving notice.
  9. 8.6 The chairperson shall be the one appointed and secretary of the Disciplinary shall be elected amongst members themselves.
  10. 8.7 Functions and Duties of the Disciplinary Committee:
  11. 8.7.1 Disciplinary Committee shall have original jurisdiction over all NSU matters.
  12. 8.7.2 The Disciplinary Committee shall prescribe a code of conduct to be followed by all NSU members.
  13. 8.7.3 The Disciplinary Committee shall exercise powers to discipline students organisation(s) found engaging in activities detrimental to the interests of the student body and the College as a whole. Subject to these provisions:
  14. 8.7.3.1 The Disciplinary Committee may advise NSU executive to recommend banning of an organisation to the Students Affairs Officer.
  15. 8.7.3.2 Where an organisation with reasonable membership other than the executive alone is found with a case where the penalty is banning, such banning shall only be imposed on the executive of such an organisation.
  16. 8.7.3.3 Where an organisation is not satisfied with the ruling of the Disciplinary, it reserves the right to appeal directly to the NSU President and/or Students Affairs Officer.
  17. 8.7.3.4 The Disciplinary Committee shall decide on the interpretation of the constitution of NSU and other constitutions submitted to NSU by students' organisation applying for recognition.
  18. 8.7.3.5 In case of a dispute over the interpretation of any clause of NSU or any other student organisation's constitution, the Disciplinary shall be empowered to interpret such clause(s).
  19. 8.8 Rules of the Disciplinary Committee:
  20. 8.8.1 Without prejudice to the provisions of the College disciplinary regulation, the Disciplinary Committee may summon a student (hereinafter called the accused/defendant) to appear before it in respect of the following:
    Disruption of a student body meeting.
    Any behaviour at student body meetings, sports functions etc. tending to bring discredit to the College.
    Any improper behaviour within the precincts of the College, usage of abusive language, fighting, stealing and any other behaviour the complainant may deem unacceptable to their own subjective judgement.
    Wilful damage to or defacement of any property belonging to or under the control of NSU or the College.
    General misadministration prejudicial to student interests.
  21. 8.8.2 The chairperson of the Disciplinary shall inform the accused or defendant against whom a complaint has been made of the charges in writing at least two (2) days before the date of the hearing.
  22. 8.8.3 After hearing all evidence, the Disciplinary Committee shall submit its order as regards the action to be taken against any student or organisation.
  23. 8.8.4 A member of the Disciplinary Committee shall not sit on the Disciplinary (Bench) for a particular matter if:
    a) He/she has any personal interest whatsoever in the matter before the Disciplinary Committee, or
    b) He/she is required or wishes to give any evidence relating to the matter before the Disciplinary Committee.
  24. 8.8.5 Five members of the Disciplinary Committee shall form a quorum.
  25. 8.8.6 On failure to comply with a sentence imposed by the Disciplinary Committee, the accused/defendant shall be referred to the Students Affairs Officer who shall deal with the case provided for under disciplinary regulations of the Northern Technical College.
  26. 8.8.7 Subject to the provisions of this constitution, the Disciplinary may regulate its own procedures by rules formulated by itself.
  27. 8.8.8 The Disciplinary Committee shall have the authority to withdraw all or part of the privileges of membership for a stated period and request compensation from an offender. The authority vested in this paragraph is subject to appeal by the defendant to NSU President in two (2) working days.
  28. 8.8.9 The Disciplinary shall use its discretion to decide upon urgent matters restraining an organ of NSU from going ahead with an action it deems unfit or unfair.
  29. 8.8.10 The Disciplinary shall be the highest body in all disciplinary matters pertaining to all students' affairs and/or conflicts. Subject to this provision all disputes and conflicts amongst union members or union organs shall be referred to the arbitration of the Disciplinary.

Article 9: Hostel Representatives

  1. 9.1 There shall be Hostel Co-ordinators.
  2. 9.2 The Hostel Co-ordinators shall implement College and NSU policy at hostel level.
  3. 9.3 Composition: Each Hostel Block on campus shall have a Block representative appointed by the Warden/Matron in liaison with the Hostels co-ordinator.
  4. 9.4 Block Representatives shall:
  5. 9.4.1 Settle dispute between block or floor mates.
  6. 9.4.2 Report all cases of indiscipline at block or floor level to the Hostel Co-ordinator.
  7. 9.4.3 Report all faults to relevant authorities i.e. the Hostels co-ordinator.
  8. 9.4.4 Liaise with Hostels Co-ordinator to ensure high levels of cleanliness.
  9. 9.4.5 The Block Representatives shall perform any other functions deemed fit to improve the welfare of students at block level.
  10. 9.5 Hostel Co-ordinator shall:
  11. 9.5.1 Sit on the Union Executive.
  12. 9.5.2 Implement NSU policy at hostel level.
  13. 9.5.3 Supervise Block representatives and review their work progress.
  14. 9.5.4 Delegate their duties to representatives where necessary.

Article 10: Committees

  1. 10.1 Finance and Investments Committee
  2. 10.1.1 Shall be responsible for the administration of the buildings entrusted to Union by Nortec Management and the maintenance of those amenities.
  3. 10.1.2 To scrutinise all applicants for business premises under NSU.
  4. 10.1.3 Shall ensure that all businesses on campus are service oriented by regulating prices of commodities and services.
  5. 10.1.4 To initiate fund-raising ventures/investment projects.
  6. 10.1.5 Finance and investments committee shall be comprised of five members as follows:
    The Chairperson i.e. Treasurer General
    The secretary
    Three committee members
    Shall sit twice per month to review their progress.
    One member staff appointed by management.
  7. 10.2 Academic and Student Welfare Committee
  8. 10.2.1 The committee chaired by the Academic and Student Welfare Co-ordinator shall comprise of two class representatives from each program.
  9. 10.2.2 To support the work of all Union officers involved in Academic and welfare work.
  10. 10.2.3 To advise Union Executive on policy issues regarding Academic and welfare.
  11. 10.2.4 To receive reports from those representatives whose duties cover issues of education and welfare and to hold those officers to account.
  12. 10.2.5 Shall sit twice per month to review their progress.
  13. 10.3 Recreation and Entertainment Committee
  14. 10.3.1 The Committee shall be chaired by the Entertainment Coordinator and his duties shall be:
  15. 10.3.1 Shall implement NSU policy on recreation and entertainment.
  16. 10.3.2 Shall make recommendations to NSU Executive on how to enhance recreation and entertainment for students to acceptable College standards.
  17. 10.3.3 To formulate policy on recreation and entertainment and see to it that the Union executive implements such policies.
  18. 10.3.4 Shall be comprised of five members as follows:
    The Chairperson i.e. Entertainment Coordinator.
    The secretary.
    Two members from each department, i.e., one senior and one first year student.
    Three students appointed by NSU.
    Shall sit twice per month to review their progress.
  19. 10.4 Security Committee
  20. 10.4.1 Shall make recommendations to the College security wing on how to enhance security.
  21. 10.4.2 Shall have the power to report any suspected person to the relevant authority (i.e. chief security).
  22. 10.4.3 Shall liaise with the security wing to investigate theft on campus.
  23. 10.4.4 Shall have the right to question anyone they suspect to be a non-student found loitering on campus.
  24. 10.4.5 Shall be comprised of five members as follows:
    The Chairperson who shall be the college security officer.
    The secretary - Matron/Warden.
    Four committee members.
    Shall sit twice per month to review their progress.
  25. 10.5 Health and Sanitation Committee
  26. 10.5.1 Shall inspect all health and sanitation facilities and make recommendations to NSU executive who shall table such recommendation before the College administration. Such facilities shall include among others, the ablution blocks and business premises.
  27. 10.5.2 Shall spearhead health campaigns on campus in liaison with management.
  28. 10.5.3 Shall recommend to NSU executive to call on professional health inspectors upon serving notice to the College administration to inspect any student service facility in the College.
  29. 10.6 Sports Committee
  30. 10.6.1 The Committee shall be headed by the elected sports coordinator and duties shall be:
  31. 10.6.1 To formulate policies on sports and see to it that the Union Executive implements such policies.
  32. 10.6.2 To make recommendations to NSU Executive on how to enhance sports for students to acceptable College standards.
  33. 10.6.3 Shall comprise of chairpersons/captains of all sporting clubs/teams on campus.
  34. 10.6.4 To encourage and co-ordinate all sporting activities on campus in liaison with the College Sports Officer.
  35. 10.6.5 To prepare and through the Sports Coordinator present to the Secretary General an annual report of all sporting activities undertaken by all member clubs 14 days before NSU Annual General Meetings (AGM).
  36. 10.6.6 Shall sit twice per month to review their progress.
  37. 10.6.7 The Coordinator shall be the custodian of every sport equipment which shall be handed over to the sports officer at the end of every term.

Article 11: The Union Executive

  1. There shall be a Union Executive elected directly by the students and which shall be responsible for the day-to-day conduct of Union Affairs.
  2. 11.1 Composition:
  3. 11.1.1 President
  4. 11.1.2 Vice-President
  5. 11.1.3 Secretary General
  6. 11.1.4 Treasurer General
  7. 11.1.5 Vice Treasurer General
  8. 11.1.6 Publicity Secretary General
  9. 11.1.7 Academic and Welfare Coordinator
  10. 11.1.8 Sports Coordinator
  11. 11.1.9 Hostel Coordinators (i.e. two at Kwacha and two at Chela)
  12. 11.1.10 Entertainment Coordinator
  13. 11.1.11 Library Coordinator
  14. 11.2 Functions and duties of the executive
  15. 11.2.1 To implement policies sanctioned by the General Meeting.
  16. 11.2.2 In the event of the Union Executive considering a matter, which is not bound by the existing policy directive, the Union Executive may determine a policy.
  17. 11.2.3 The Union Executive shall remain in active office until the next appointment of the Interim Union Executive and upon hand over and signing for ceremoniously.
  18. 11.2.4 The new Interim Executive shall be oriented into office a week before hand over by the Union Executive.
  19. 11.2.5 The Union Executive may subject to the provision of this constitution recommend to the college Disciplinary Committee, suspension, removal or dismissal of any member of the Executive if dishonest, inefficient, incompetent or has refused or failed to carry out the lawful decisions or directions of the Executive Committee or is guilty of conduct prejudicial to the interests of the Union. The Disciplinary Committee shall determine the recommendations in a reasonable period of time upon receiving the matter.
  20. 11.2.6 No person who has been convicted of any case of dishonesty either by courts of law or College Disciplinary Committee or has serious criminal matters pending or is insolvent or declared bankrupt or of unsound mind may be elected or appointed to any office in the Union Executive. When the above contingencies occur, the officer shall be removed from office.
  21. 11.2.7 The Union Executive shall remain non-partisan and not actively involved in Politics.
  22. 11.2.8 Only fully paid up students shall stand for elections.

Article 12: Powers and Duties of Union Executive Officers

12.1 The President

  1. 12.1.1 Shall be the Chief Executive Officer of NSU and shall have powers of supervision and direction of and over the affairs of NSU executive and its Executive officers.
  2. 12.1.2 Shall preside at all meetings of NSU Executive body and shall be responsible for proper conduct of business of such meetings.
  3. 12.1.3 The President shall be an Ex-Officio member of all student committees and student organizations registered with the Students Affairs Officer.
  4. 12.1.4 Represent the Union and student body at all official functions.
  5. 12.1.5 Sign and issue all official statements concerning the Union with the Secretary General.
  6. 12.1.6 Be a member of Student Assessment Committee of the Northern Technical College.
  7. 12.1.7 Take a leadership role in major campaigns, which are designed to promote the interests of the Union and/or its members.
  8. 12.1.8 To act as principal channel of communication between the Union and external bodies.
  9. 12.1.9 Shall in conjunction with the Secretary General be responsible to the Students' body, for proper maintenance of good order and efficiency of NSU and shall endeavour to secure observance of these rules by all members of the Union and the Executive.
  10. 12.1.10 May remain on campus during all vacations and all closures dependent on the availability of NSU funds.
  11. 12.1.11 The President shall have such powers as are incidental to the performance of the duties mentioned above.

12.2 The Vice President

  1. 12.2.1 Shall take on responsibilities of the President if he/she is unable to do so. In the event of a vacancy occurring in the office of the President, he/she shall assume the duties and authority of that office until the by-elections are held.
  2. 12.2.2 Be responsible for the discipline of Executive members and to sit on the college Disciplinary Committee established for student's discipline in the Students Affairs Office.
  3. 12.2.3 Be responsible for handling the affairs of foreign students and to maintain contacts with the outside world.
  4. 12.2.4 Ensure a positive image of Union Executive and the entire Union.
  5. 12.2.5 Establish links and maintain cordial relations with students of other Higher institutions of learning within and outside the Republic of Zambia.
  6. 12.2.6 Liaise with Students Affairs Officer with a view to securing benefits such as discounts at stores, student nights (Nites), reduced travel fares and such other concessions for the benefit of students.
  7. 12.2.7 In addition to such powers, functions and duties as are assigned in this Constitution, the Vice-President shall act for the President in such matters as the President may within his own powers delegate to him/her.

12.3 Secretary General

  1. 12.3.1 Shall be the Chief Administrator of the Union and be responsible for the conduct of the affairs of the Union in accordance with these rules and shall carry into effect the policy of the Union as laid down by the General Meeting.
  2. 12.3.2 Shall act as President in the absence of the President and his or her Vice.
  3. 12.3.3 Shall receive monthly reports from each officer of the Union Executive on the operations of their respective areas.
  4. 12.3.4 With the sanction of the whole Union Executive, shall recommend to the NORTEC management the banning or suspension of those publications found engaging in activities detrimental to the interests of the student body and the College as a whole.
  5. 12.3.5 Shall be responsible for record keeping i.e. a register of all members of the Union and an inventory of Union property.
  6. 12.3.6 Shall ensure that the operations of the Union are in accordance with the Laws of the Republic of Zambia.
  7. 12.3.7 Shall be responsible for informing clubs and societies of Union facilities and administering the use of notice board.
  8. 12.3.8 To consult and engage a legal representative on behalf of the Union with the approval of the entire Executive.
  9. 12.3.9 May remain on campus during all Vacations and all closures dependent on the availability of NSU funds when need arises.
  10. 12.3.10 Shall sit in the SAC meeting.

12.4 Treasurer General / Vice Treasurer General

  1. 12.4.1 The Treasurer General shall be the custodian of NSU funds. Subject to these provisions he/she:
  2. 12.4.2 Shall be responsible for proper and accrued accounts of receipts and expenditures of NSU.
  3. 12.4.3 Shall disburse funds of the union as per policy laid down by union.
  4. 12.4.4 Shall prepare termly financial report during his or her tenure of office and shall present financial report at the Annual General Meeting (AGM) provided that such reports shall be put in writing and made available to 2/3 of student members and College Management, 7 days before presentation at a meeting.
  5. 12.4.5 Shall prepare and present termly budget reports to the full Union Executive at the beginning of every term.
  6. 12.4.6 May remain on campus during long vacations and all closures, except for short vacations dependent on the availability of NSU funds or when need arises.
  7. 12.4.7 The Vice Treasurer General shall work with the Treasurer General in executing the duties.

12.5 Publicity Secretary General

  1. 12.5.1 Shall be chief spokesperson of the students of the Northern Technical College.
  2. 12.5.2 In conjunction with the Secretary General give notices of executive and students meeting.
  3. 12.5.3 Shall file all documents that are stuck on all notice boards in the college.
  4. 12.5.4 Shall chair the editorial board of any union publication in the college.
  5. 12.5.5 Shall be responsible for releasing and disseminating all official pronouncements on behalf of the union on policies and activities.
  6. 12.5.6 Shall assume functions of the Secretary General in his absence.
  7. 12.5.7 Shall remain on campus during long vacations and all closures, except for short vacations dependent on the availability of NSU funds when need arises.
  8. 12.5.8 To arrange the development, marketing and promotion of Union commercial services.
  9. 12.5.9 Shall have full responsibility for dissemination of Union information and publicity across campus; she/he shall keep the Chairperson of Students Council and President and the student body informed on all matters of the Union.
  10. 12.5.10 To create and utilise a magazine/publication as a springboard for idea sharing and debating issues affecting students and the nation at large.

12.6 The Academic and Student Welfare Coordinator

  1. 12.6.1 Shall co-ordinate all student academic affairs.
  2. 12.6.2 Shall be the chairperson of the Academic and Welfare Committee.
  3. 12.6.3 Shall be the recipient of student's academic complaints.
  4. 12.6.4 Shall liaise with Library, Computer Centre and Heads of Departments to uphold standards, services and conditions of such facilities. Subject to this provision, he or she shall be responsible for negotiating for relevant literature.
  5. 12.6.5 Shall in conjunction with Head of Department, be responsible for the negotiation of research, project and other financial support.
  6. 12.6.6 The academic shall be a member of SAC Committee and shall remain on campus during long vacations and all closures except for short vacations dependent on NSU funds when need arises.

12.7 Sports Coordinator

He shall be the head of the Sports Committee and shall perform duties as stated under the Sports Committee.

12.8 Hostels Coordinator

He shall be the head of the Block Representatives. He/she shall perform duties as stated under Hostel Representatives.

12.9 Entertainment Coordinator

(As per Recreation and Entertainment Committee)

12.10 Library Coordinator

She/he shall be the head of the Library Committee and shall keep the Union Executive informed of the conditions and services at the library in the interests of students. He/she shall work hand in hand with Academic and Student Welfare Coordinator. Shall with the help of Library assistants ensure the Library is open till the accepted time for closure.

Article 13: Removal From Office of a Member or the Whole NSU Executive

  1. 13.1 Any number of students shall petition for a removal of a member or the entire NSU Executive. They shall collect 50% + 1 signatures from full members of the Union. Such a petition shall be tabled before the Student's Affairs Officer and reasons shall be given for the same before it.
  2. 13.2 An individual member shall cease to hold office if and when:
  3. 13.2.1 They die.
  4. 13.2.2 They cease to be full-time registered students of the Northern Technical College.
  5. 13.2.3 They tender their resignation in writing, such resignation to be effective on the expiration of seven (7) days after the delivery of the notice to fellow executive members.
  6. 13.2.4 They absent themselves from executive (including general meeting where applicable) meetings for two (2) times in succession without leave or reasonable cause.
  7. 13.2.5 They fail without reasonable cause to carry out such duties as may be entrusted to them as members of the executive.
  8. 13.2.6 They are mentally or physically incapable of performing their duties.
  9. 13.2.7 They are partisan and actively in party politics.
  10. 13.2.8 They are found guilty of contravening the code of conduct prescribed by the NSU Executives.
  11. 13.3 Whenever the Union Executive has been removed from office, in accordance with the foregoing, there shall be five students appointed by the Student Affairs Officer to run the affairs of the Union Executive.
  12. 13.4 Fresh election shall be held within 30 days of the impeachment of the Union Executive.

Article 14: The Interim Executive

  1. 14.1 The outgoing NSU executive shall within 30 days before they write their final examinations constitute an interim executive (whose term of office is one (1) Term) consisting of eight (12) persons. These shall be:
  2. President
  3. Secretary General
  4. Treasurer General
  5. Publicity Secretary General
  6. Academic and Welfare Coordinator
  7. Sports Coordinator
  8. Hostel Coordinator (4)
  9. Recreation and Entertainment Coordinator (2)
  10. Disciplinary Coordinator (4)
  11. 14.2 Functions and Duties of the Interim Executive
  12. 14.2.1 To implement policies sanctioned by the Students General Assembly and the General Meeting.
  13. 14.2.2 In the event of the Interim Union Executive considering a matter, which is not bound by the existing policy directive, the Union Executive may determine policy for subsequent ratification by the Students General Assembly.
  14. 14.2.3 The Interim Union Executive shall remain in active office until the next Union elections.
  15. 14.2.4 The Interim Union Executive may subject to the provision of this constitution recommend to the college Disciplinary committee, suspension, removal or dismissal of any member of the Executive if dishonest, inefficient, incompetent or has refused or failed to carry out the lawful decisions or directions of the Executive Committee or is guilty of conduct prejudicial to the interests of the Union. The Disciplinary committee shall determine the recommendations in a reasonable period of time upon receiving the matter.
  16. 14.2.5 No person who has been convicted of any case of dishonesty either by courts of law or College Disciplinary Committee or has serious criminal matters pending or is insolvent or declared bankrupt or of unsound mind may be appointed to any office in the Interim Union Executive. When the above contingencies occur, the officer shall be removed from office.
  17. 14.2.6 The Interim Union Executive shall remain non-partisan and not actively involved in party politics.
  18. 14.2.7 Only fully paid up students shall be appointed.

Article 15: Elections

  1. 15.1 There shall be an Electoral Commission of NSU appointed at the beginning of the third term of each academic year, among students of Nortec and not exceeding 5 in number that will work with the help of SAO.
  2. 15.2 The Student's Affairs Officer shall institute the interviewing panel for the Electoral Commission. The interviewing panel shall comprise of:
  3. 15.2.1 One member of staff nominated by the NORTEC management.
  4. 15.2.2 One member of interim NSU Executive.
  5. 15.2.3 One representative of Christian organisation.
  6. 15.2.4 Academic Welfare Coordinator.
  7. 15.2.5 Students Counsellor.
  8. 15.2.6 The Students Affairs Officer may appoint among his/her staff member of staff.
  9. 15.2.7 One representative from disciplinary.
  10. 15.3 The Chairperson and Secretary of the Electoral commission shall be elected by fellow Commissioners from amongst themselves.
  11. 15.4 A person shall not be qualified for appointment as a member of the Commission Electoral if she/he holds a portfolio in the Union Executive or the Students' council.
  12. 15.5 The electoral commission may subject to the provision of this constitution recommend to the Disciplinary Committee, suspension, removal or dismissal of any member of the electoral commission if dishonest, inefficient, incompetent or has refused or failed to carry out the lawful decisions or directions of the Electoral Committee or is guilty of conduct prejudicial to the interests of the students. The Disciplinary Committee shall determine the recommendations in a reasonable period of time upon receiving the matter.
  13. 15.6 Once appointed to the Electoral Commission, a person shall not run for office in the Union for that particular year.
  14. 15.7 If a vacancy occurs on the Electoral Commission, the Chairperson of Student Council in collaboration with the Students Affairs Officer may fill the gap by appointing a person to the Commission, qualified for such an appointment.
  15. 15.8 The Commission shall upon taking office assume full powers of conducting and regulating Union elections.
  16. 15.9 The Electoral Commission shall be empowered to disqualify those candidates who:
  17. 15.9.1 Engage in character assassination of other candidates.
  18. 15.9.2 Whose acts shall be seen by the Commission to be prejudicial to other candidates.
  19. 15.9.3 Have criminal records with the Police, College Security or Students Affairs Officer.
  20. 15.9.4 Have been expelled from the Union Executive and those once suspended but not cleared by the Disciplinary Committee of the College.
  21. 15.9.5 Whose campaign managers or members of the campaign team indulge in drunkenness and disorder and use of abusive language during the campaign.
  22. 15.9.6 Fail to follow laid down procedures.
  23. 15.9.7 Whose campaign crew fails to follow laid down procedures as may be revised by the Electoral Commission from time to time.
  24. 15.10 The Commission shall draw rules for the conduct of election without diverting from the generality of the foregoing.
  25. 15.11 The Commission shall remain in active position up to the end of the first term of the following academic year, and shall have the powers to conduct any by-elections as need arises.
  26. 15.12 The NSU executive general elections shall be held five [3] weeks before the end of the third term.
  27. 15.13 The electoral commission shall declare unopposed candidates as duly elected.
  28. 15.14 Elections to NSU executive shall be by secret ballot.
  29. 15.15 No person shall be elected to the Union Executive if she/he is a Graduating, part-time, or ODFL student.
  30. 15.16 The new NSU executive shall assume office within 7 days of the confirmation of elections results at a public meeting to be attended by the Students Affairs Officer, which shall be convened by the Electoral commission. The outgoing interim executive shall finally hand over to the incoming executive the keys, records, documents, inventory list, accounts and any other documents relating to the administration of the executive of NSU. At the said public meeting the outgoing and incoming presidents of NSU shall sign a declaration certifying what has been handed over and received represented the total of all assets and records of NSU at the time of declaration.
  31. 15.17 Whenever an individual member of NSU executive ceases to hold office, the NSU Executive shall propose a candidate who shall be scrutinised by Student's Affairs Officer with immediate effect.
  32. 15.18 In the case of a by-election, the electoral commission shall put in place necessary logistics for holding the same by-elections.

Article 16: Finances

  1. 16.1 Contributions to NSU finances
  2. All students shall be liable to contribute to NSU account. Such contributions shall be at the beginning of each term and as decided upon by NSU executive.
  3. 16.1.1 All contributions to NSU Account shall be made upon registration through the College Administration.
  4. 16.1.2 The Union may receive a sum of money by way of grant, donation or in any other way from any source provided it is given in good faith and in the interest of the College.
  5. 16.2 Maintenance and application of NSU funds
  6. 16.2.1 The following shall be signatories to NSU account:
    The President
    Treasurer General
    Students Affairs Officer
    One appointed member
  7. 16.2.2 All NSU funds shall be held, used and administered in trust for the benefit of the whole student body, and no personal loans or disbursements shall be made to any individual(s).
  8. 16.2.3 The NSU Executive shall account collectively and bear responsibility for misappropriation of funds, unless individual executive members exculpate themselves to the satisfaction of the disciplinary committee.
  9. 16.2.4 Any student who misappropriates funds shall be handed over to the College disciplinary committee.
  10. 16.2.5 No payment of NSU monies shall be made without the written authorization of the Treasurer General and one other signatory. All cheque payments of the Union shall not be valid unless a payment voucher duly signed by three (3) signatories.
  11. 16.2.6 The Executive in accordance with the policy of General Meeting shall invest any funds, which in the opinion of the Union Executive are not immediately required for the purposes of the Union.
  12. 16.2.7 All NSU leaders whom this constitution empowers to remain on campus during vacations and closures shall be paid an allowance at the rate of 30% of current boarding fees. Subject to this provision, only the President, the Secretary General, Treasurer General, Publicity Secretary, the Academic and Student Welfare Coordinator and two hostel co-ordinators shall be paid allowances during short vacations (herein called short breaks) depending on the availability of NSU funds.
  13. 16.2.8 There shall be a sitting allowance paid to all NSU Executive members which shall be reviewed by NSU executive from time to time.
  14. 16.2.9 Allowances shall be paid to the Constitution Review Commission whenever they sit for such an activity.

Article 17: Clubs, Associations and Societies

  1. 17.1 All clubs, Associations and Societies (hereinafter referred to as student organizations) shall be registered with the Students Affairs Office subject to:
  2. 17.1.1 Recommendation by the NSU Executive.
  3. 17.1.2 Payment to the Union a registration fee decided by the NSU Executive.
  4. 17.2 Any student organization to be registered must:
  5. 17.2.1 Present a minimum of ten (10) founding members, except for publications where five (5) members of the editorial board shall be presented.
  6. 17.2.2 Present its constitution to be approved by the NSU Executive.
  7. 17.3 All student organizations shall furnish the Students Affairs Officer and NSU Executive with the names of their executive members at the beginning of every term and whenever they hold elections.
  8. 17.4 All student organizations shall pay nominal renewal fees at the beginning of every term determined by the NSU Executive. Subject to the provisions of these articles, all monies paid by student organizations shall be used to the benefit of such organizations.
  9. 17.5 Where a student organization is identified with a particular function or event, no other student organization shall carry out the function or event under the same name unless where they enter into an agreement to hold it jointly or otherwise.
  10. 17.6 All disciplinary matters of student organizations shall be tabled before the Disciplinary Committee.
  11. 17.7 All student organisations shall give notice of activities they want to carry out to the NSU executive and give a report at every end of the term.
  12. 17.8 All student organisations shall seek prior permission from the Union Executive before inviting a professional into the College.

Article 18: Publications

  1. 18.1 Any recognized student organization may have its own publication(s).
  2. 18.2 NSU shall be held responsible for any matter contained in such publications.
  3. 18.3 NSU Executive shall in no way make regulations that will derogate from freedom of the press or freedom of expression of campus publications provided the said publication follow the general guidelines as provided by the Students Affairs Officer at registration of a publication.

Article 19: Affiliation to Other Organisations

  1. 19.1 The Union shall be affiliated to any non-political organisation if such affiliation is in the interest of the student body.
  2. 19.2 A referendum shall be conducted to get the student's opinion before such affiliation shall be effected or withdrawn.

Article 20: Legal Assistance

The NSU executive shall have power to provide or contribute towards legal advice and assistance for any member of the Union whose subscriptions are fully paid on any matter connected with his/her academic progress, provided that the necessity of the legal assistance is not occasioned by unlawful acts or omissions, drunkenness, drug addiction, wilful negligence or misconduct.

Article 21: Amendments and Review

  1. 21.1 A minimum of 2/3 of students shall have the right to petition the Students Affairs Office to amend any part of this constitution. The SAO shall liaise with the union executive after which a constitution review commission shall be appointed.
  2. 21.2 The student body shall approve the amendments to the constitution.

Article 22: Adoption

  1. 22.1 The student populace (2/3) shall adopt the Constitution by writing their names and appending their signature against their names. There shall be a general meeting attended by the Principal or his representative(s) and the Students Affairs Officer at which the final adoption shall be done.
  2. 22.2 Upon adoption, all offices created by this constitution shall become effective and any offices not in this constitution cease to exist subject to the provisions of this constitution.
  3. 22.3 This constitution shall be signed on behalf of the students by the current NSU President and Secretary General, witnessed by the College Principal and Students Affairs Officer.
